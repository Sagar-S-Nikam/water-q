Water Quality Parameters

Three key parameters measured against WHO (World Health Organization) drinking water standards.

⚗️

pH Level

pH stands for Potential of Hydrogen. It measures how acidic or alkaline the water is on a scale of 0 to 14.

A pH below 7 means the water is acidic — it can corrode pipes and taste sour. A pH above 7 means it is alkaline — it can cause scale build-up. Neutral (pH 7) is pure water.

✅ Safe Range: 6.5 – 8.5
🌫️

Turbidity

Turbidity measures the cloudiness or haziness of water caused by suspended particles like dirt, bacteria, algae, or silt.

High turbidity means the water looks muddy or milky. It can indicate the presence of harmful microorganisms and makes water unsafe to drink. Measured in NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Units).

✅ Safe Range: ≤ 5.0 NTU
🧂

TDS (Total Dissolved Solids)

TDS measures the total amount of dissolved minerals, salts, metals, and other substances in water, such as calcium, magnesium, sodium, and chloride.

Very high TDS makes water taste salty or bitter and may contain harmful heavy metals. Very low TDS can also be undesirable as it lacks essential minerals. Measured in mg/L (milligrams per litre).

✅ Safe Range: ≤ 500 mg/L

How Status is Determined

The system checks all three parameters together for every reading:

✅

SAFE

All 3 parameters are within safe range. Water is safe to use.

⚠️

DANGER

Even ONE parameter is out of range. Water quality alert triggered.
